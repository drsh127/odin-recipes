Chocolate Zucchini Muffins

Description

I tend to lean towards muffins over a loaf simply for self control and especially when chocolate is involved. These are the muffins I will be eating all summer long. They are dark and rich and unbelievably moist from the zucchini and almond butter. They are also grain free, sweetened with maple syrup and sprinkled with salt. They are the perfect excuse to eat vegetables while indulging a chocolate craving!

Ingredients

  • coconut oil or avocado spray for greasing the tin
  • 1 cup almond butter
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup (I love Runamok Cocoa Bean infused syrup)
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups of shredded zucchini, about 2 small
  • 1/2 cup plus more for topping- dark chocolate chips or chopped chocolate
  • flaky salt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Grease or line a muffin tin.
  2. In a large bowl combine the almond butter, maple syrup, cacao powder, salt, egg, vanilla, and baking soda until smooth.
  3. Gently squeeze out the excess liquid from the grated zucchini. Stir in zucchini and chocolate chips.
  4. Pour into muffin tins and generously top with more chocolate and a sprinkle of salt. Bake for 30-35 minutes for jumbo muffins and 20-25 for a standard.
